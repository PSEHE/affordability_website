### Related Tools & Resources

Other tools and resources for energy affordability and equity analysis.

#### Energy Burden & Affordability: Metrics

- [US Department of Energy Low-income Energy Affordability Data (LEAD) Tool](https://www.energy.gov/scep/slsc/lead-tool) — Models energy costs and cost burdens at different geographic levels (national, state, county, and city) across the US. LEAD breaks this data down by fuel type, demographics, household characteristics (income metrics as well as resident tenure), and building characteristics (age, type, primary heating fuel). Data comes primarily from US Census Bureau American Community Survey 2022 Public Use Microdata samples, and US EIA 2022 electric utility (Survey Form-861) and gas utility (Survey Form-176) data. Provides similar breakdowns compared to NEAT, but NEAT simulates data based on the same sources for every household in the US, and provides additional metrics, like the Energy Affordability Gap, which can be more actionable for policymakers and community groups.
- [California Energy Commission Deep Dive into Energy Affordability](https://www.energy.ca.gov/data-reports/data-exploration-tools/energy-equity-indicators-dashboard-collection/deep-dive-energy) — A comprehensive interactive tool assessing energy affordability in California, measured through metrics like energy burden, Energy Affordability Gap, and the Energy Affordability Index. Provides multiple geographic levels, from census tracts to utility service territories, and includes multiple filters for community types (e.g. housing tenure, disadvantaged communities, least affordable quartile of census tracts, etc.). In-home energy data sourced from the DOE's LEAD tool, and supplemented with transportation data from CalTrans and CEC. Further demographic data comes from the American Community Survey and the University of Washington's Center for Women's Welfare.

#### Energy Burden & Affordability: Relevant Policies

- [Just Solutions Energy Affordability Policy Library](https://statesleadtheway.justsolutionscollective.org/the-policy-library/) — A comprehensive collection of resources, regulations, and policies to reduce home electricity and gas costs. Users can browse through the library, or filter by Affordability Strategy (e.g. Utility Reforms), Region (Federal, State, Local), Policy Mechanism (e.g. Legislation), Keywords (e.g. Air Quality), and Community Benefits (e.g. Affordability).
- [State Climate Policy Dashboard](https://www.climatepolicydashboard.org/) — An in-depth list of climate programs for each state in the US. Users can filter for policies by topic, including affordability-related areas like Electrification, Buildings and Efficiency, and Transportation. The dashboard also divides programs up by states that have enacted them, states that have not, and states that are in the process of enacting them.
- [Energy Justice Disconnections Dashboard](https://energyjustice.indiana.edu/disconnection-dashboard/index.html) — A tool to identify utility disconnections across the US. Can be used to assess disconnection trends across different geographies, information on utility companies, and state policies that aim to protect customers. Data is updated through 2024, but it should be noted that this tool is not yet finalized.
- [Energy Equity Project](https://energyequityproject.com/) — Provides a hub of resources for frontline communities and partners to advocate against shutoffs. Includes information on lessons learned, how to hone arguments, a boilerplate language library, shutoff protection guidance, and more.
- [ACEEE City Clean Energy Scorecard](https://www.aceee.org/city-clean-energy-scorecard) - Tracks policies across 75 of the most populous cities in the US to assess progress toward energy and climate goals, primarily through energy efficiency and building performance standards. Also examines programs that advance racial and social equity with regard to building energy efficiency.
- [NEADA Energy Hardship Project](https://neada.org/energy-affordability-project/) - Tracks EIA energy price projections and calculates how much the average family's bills can be paid for by LIHEAP grants in the coming years.
- [Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency (DSIRE)](https://dsireusa.org/) - A nationwide database of state-level renewable energy and efficiency incentives and rebates.
- [NEEP Regional Policy Roundup](https://neep.org/public-policy-and-programs/regional-roundup) - Snapshot of state policies for energy efficiency specifically in the Northeastern 12 states (CT, MD, NY, WV, MA, PA, DE, NH, RI, ME, NJ, VT) and D.C. Categories include utility planning and regulation, codes and standards, equity and workforce, and carbon reduction obligations.

#### Environmental Justice

- [Climate and Economic Justice Screening Tool (CEJST)](https://public-environmental-data-partners.github.io/j40-cejst-2/en/#3/37.09/-101.55) — Highlights marginalized and underinvested census tracts across the entire US. Provides population data about each tract upon selection, as well as reasons a tract is classified as disadvantaged (under categories of climate change, energy, health, housing, legacy pollution, transportation, water/wastewater, and workforce development).
- [EPA EJScreen](https://www.epa.gov/ejscreen) — Environmental justice mapping tool enabling users to assess cumulative EJ burdens at the tract level for the entire US. Taken down in January 2025 but mirrors are hosted online.

**State-level Environmental Justice Screening Tools** Tools that display a number of environmental, socioeconomic, and health endpoints by census tract in the state, as well as overall scores for environmental justice. Can be used to identify areas of the state with a combination of equity and environmental justice burdens.

- California — [CalEnviroScreen](https://oehha.ca.gov/calenviroscreen/report/calenviroscreen-40)
- Michigan — [MiEJScreen](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/maps-data/miejscreen)
- Maryland — [MDEnviroScreen](https://mde.maryland.gov/Environmental_Justice/Pages/MDEnviroScreen.aspx)
- New York — [Disadvantaged Communities Criteria](https://climate.ny.gov/Resources/Disadvantaged-Communities-Criteria)
- Washington — [Environmental Health Disparities Map](https://doh.wa.gov/data-and-statistical-reports/washington-tracking-network-wtn/washington-environmental-health-disparities-map)

#### Building & Energy Data

- [NREL ResStock](https://resstock.nrel.gov/) — Residential building stock analysis. Physics-based simulations of a diverse array of households that may not match observed consumption patterns, since they do not account for behaviors to reduce energy consumption.
- [EIA Residential Energy Consumption Survey (RECS)](https://www.eia.gov/consumption/residential/) — Energy consumption, cost, and housing data from a nationally representative sample of housing units.
- [Lawrence Berkeley Lab US Distributed Solar and Storage Tool](https://emp.lbl.gov/tracking-sun-tool) — Tracks solar and storage installations across all states over time, including system design, install prices, market characteristics, and income.
