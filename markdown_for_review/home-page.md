## The Scale of the Challenge

In 2022, American households spent billions on energy to power and heat their homes. Low-income households bear a disproportionate share of this burden.

- **$285 billion** spent annually on residential electricity and heating fuels
- **Two-thirds** of low-income households exceed the 6% affordability threshold (ACEEE, 2020)
- **Over $30 billion** needed annually to ensure no household spends more than six percent of income on home energy needs

## Powerful Analysis Tools

Our interactive dashboard provides two main capabilities for energy equity analysis:

- **Affordability Landscape:** Explore data on energy costs, bill assistance needs, emissions, and more down to the census tract. Results can be independently calculated for multiple end uses such as heating and all major energy sources and further broken down by several demographic and household characteristic filters, including race, income, heating fuel, renter or owner, and education.
- **Decision Support:** Model the impacts of efficiency programs, weatherization initiatives, electrification policies, local community solar, and more.
- **Data Export:** Download data for your own analysis and reporting.
