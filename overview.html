The Scale of the Challenge

In 2022, American households spent billions on energy to power and heat their homes. Low-income households bear a disproportionate share of this burden.

  • $285 billion spent annually on residential electricity and heating fuels
  • 67% of low-income households exceed the 6% affordability threshold (ACEEE, 2020)
  • $30 billion gap between actual costs and affordable levels

Tool Motivation

New technologies are opening the door to meeting load growth while decreasing emissions, cleaning the air, and making homes safer. This tool aims to go beyond just quantifying the affordability challenges, but help analysts quickly and directly estimate how various solutions can improve affordability. No one-size-all policy affecting household energy use would suit whole states, let alone the entire US. This highlights the importance of tailoring known policy toolkits to the local level maximize household cost savings and greenhouse gas emission reductions.


The interactive map below showcases a slice of the data from our national household dataset. Each dot represents a census tract, or about 4000 people on average. Use the slider to compare total CO2e emissions from residential energy use (by quartile) per tract, or total Energy Affordability Gap (by quartile) per census tract. Zoom in or out to examine specific areas, and click on a dot for more information about this tract (total affordability gap in dollars, total CO2e emissions in kilograms, and median energy cost burden). Areas that are in the upper quartiles for both greenhouse gas emissions and affordability gap would represent opportunities to reduce household costs and emissions simultaneously. In this example, many rural areas experience high affordability concerns AND carbon-intense residential energy, identifying the opportunity to cut costs and emissions.

Powerful Analysis Tools

Our interactive dashboard provides two main capabilities for energy equity analysis:

  • Statistics Generator: Explore data on energy rates, costs, and burdens across geographic levels (county, state, utility territory, metropolitan areas, national) with a number of demographic and household characteristic filters (race, income bracket, heating fuel used, renter or owner, education level, and more).
  • Decision Support: Model the impacts of efficiency programs, weatherization initiatives, and electrification policies, like targeted heat pump replacement, local community solar, and more.
  • Data Export: Download data for your own analysis and reporting.
