National Energy Affordability Tool Guide

A comprehensive guide to using the interactive dashboard for energy equity analysis, including the Statistics Generator and Decision Support tools.

The National Energy Affordability Tool provides two main capabilities for energy equity analysis: an Existing Affordability Landscape Tool for exploring current data, and a Decision Support Tool for modeling the impacts of policy interventions. Below is a guide to using each component of the dashboard.

01 • Statistics Generator

Existing Affordability Landscape Tool

The Existing Affordability Landscape Tool allows you to explore comprehensive data on energy costs, consumption, emissions, and affordability gaps across the United States. This section describes each component of the interface.

The Geography selector determines the spatial unit of analysis for all visualizations and statistics.

  • Nationwide: Aggregate statistics for the entire United States
  • States: All 50 states plus Washington D.C.
  • Counties: Over 3,000 U.S. counties
  • Utility Service Territories: Electric utility boundaries
  • Metropolitan Areas: Census-defined metro/micro statistical areas

The filtering panel allows you to narrow your analysis to specific household types based on demographic and housing characteristics.

  • Income Level: By AMI brackets (0-30%, 30-60%, 60-80%, 80-100%, >100%), FPL, or absolute income
  • Housing Characteristics: Building type, tenure (owner/renter), year built
  • Heating Fuel: Electricity, natural gas, propane/LPG, fuel oil, other
  • Demographics: Race/ethnicity, age, household size

The interactive map displays spatial patterns in energy affordability metrics across your selected geography.

  • Choropleth coloring: Geographic units shaded by selected metric
  • Hover information: Mouse over any region for detailed statistics
  • Click to select: Click a region to focus the analysis
  • Metrics: Energy burden, costs, affordability gap, consumption, emissions

The Summary Statistics panel provides aggregate numerical summaries for your current selection.

  • Household counts: Total households and filtered subset
  • Energy costs: Mean, median, total annual costs
  • Energy burden: Mean and median burden rates, distribution
  • Affordability gap: Total gap, households exceeding threshold

The Bar Chart visualization allows comparison of metrics across categories or geographies.

  • Choose X-axis categorical variable (geography, income group, housing type)
  • Select Y-axis metric (costs, burden, consumption, emissions)
  • Aggregation options: sum, mean, median, or count
  • Sorting and grouping controls

The Scatter Chart shows relationships between two continuous variables across geographic units.

  • Configure X and Y axis variables
  • Optionally scale point size by household count
  • Color points by categorical variable
  • Hover to identify geographic units

The Network Graph visualizes relationships and flows between entities in the energy system.

  • Utility service territory relationships
  • Energy flow patterns
  • Interactive node positioning

The Treemap displays hierarchical data as nested rectangles, useful for understanding composition.

  • Share of energy costs by fuel type
  • Household distribution by income bracket
  • Geographic contribution to total emissions
02 • Policy Modeling

Decision Support Tool

The Decision Support Tool allows you to model the impacts of energy efficiency programs, weatherization initiatives, electrification policies, and rate changes on household energy costs and emissions.

Inputs

The Input panel is where you configure the policy scenario to model, based on target geography and populations defined in the Landscape portion of the tool. Parameters are organized into accordion sections:

Rate Changes

Simulate rate modifications: increases/decreases, time-of-use, tiered rates, fixed charges.

Grid Decarbonization

Model emission intensity changes: future emission factors, renewable portfolio impacts.

Weatherization & Efficiency

Configure envelope improvements: insulation, air sealing, windows, appliance upgrades.

Heating & Cooling Interventions and Fuel Switching

Model equipment upgrades: heat pump adoption, efficiency improvements, smart thermostats.

Demand Response Programs

Simulate programs that incentivize demand-side energy-saving actions, like load shifting.

Bill Assistance Programs

Model financial assistance: discounts, credits, income-qualified rates, arrearage forgiveness.

Behind-the-Meter Solar

Calculate affordability impacts from rooftop solar.

Community Solar

Affordability impacts of community solar in specific geographies and communities.

Energy Storage (Battery)

Model savings from a home battery providing power during peak demand times.

Results

After running a scenario, the Results panel displays projected impacts.

Map
The Results Map shows spatial distribution of policy impacts:

  • Baseline vs. Scenario comparison
  • Change in energy burden, affordability gap, costs
  • Emission reductions by geography

Graph
The Results Graph provides quantitative summaries:

  • Before/After bar charts for selected metrics
  • Distribution curves showing burden shifts
  • Cost-benefit analysis outputs
  • Total savings, households benefiting, emission reductions

03 • Getting the Most from the Tool

Tips and Best Practices

For Researchers

  • Always note the data year (2022) when citing statistics
  • Document filter settings used in your analysis
  • Use the export feature for reproducible workflows

For Policymakers

  • Start with state or utility-level views for jurisdiction-specific insights
  • Compare burden rates across demographic groups to identify equity concerns
  • Use affordability gap estimates for program sizing and budget planning

For Advocates

  • Generate community-specific statistics for local advocacy
  • Visualize disparities with demographic breakdowns
  • Download charts and maps for presentations and reports
